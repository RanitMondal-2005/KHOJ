Platform Overview

About Khoj

A rule-based coordination platform connecting families, hospitals, and police to help resolve missing person cases.

The Problem

Thousands of missing person cases in India remain unresolved due to poor coordination between families, hospitals, and law enforcement.

Why Khoj was built

When a person goes missing, the family files a report at the police station. Meanwhile, an unidentified patient matching that description may already be admitted to a hospital nearby — but there is no system connecting these two pieces of information.

Khoj bridges this gap by maintaining a searchable, matched database of missing person reports and unidentified patient records — with automatic scoring to surface likely matches.

What Khoj Does

Missing Person Reports
Families register and file detailed missing person reports with physical descriptions, photos, and last known location.
Unidentified Patient Records
Hospital staff upload records for unidentified patients with estimated physical details and photos.
Automatic Matching
The matching engine compares reports against patient records and generates a confidence score. High-scoring matches are flagged.
Police Investigation Support
Police officers can browse regional cases, nearby matches, and hospital contacts as a support tool for ongoing investigations.

How the Matching Engine Works

Khoj uses a weighted rule-based scoring system — no AI, no machine learning. Every factor is transparent and explainable. A missing person report is compared against each unidentified patient record across the following criteria:

Matching Factor Points Logic
Gender 20 Exact match required
Blood Group 15 Exact match required
Age up to 15 ±3 yrs = 15, ±6 = 10, ±10 = 5
Height up to 10 ±5 cm = 10, ±10 = 6, ±15 = 3
District 10 Same district = full points
Identifying Marks up to 10 Keyword overlap scoring
Eye / Hair / Skin 5 each Exact text match
Weight up to 5 ±5 kg = 5, ±10 = 3
Total Maximum Score 100 Threshold: 40+ to be stored
